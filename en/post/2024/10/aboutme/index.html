About Me

This was written on October 11, 2024, in Dublin, Ireland.

Self Introduction

Hello, my name is Li Nansen, formerly known as Li Nan. I am from China and I am an engineer who loves technology. I have a bachelor’s degree in Mechatronics and two master’s degrees in Computer Science, focusing on Embedded Systems and Natural Language Processing. I worked as a Go Language Engineer at a large internet company in China, and I am currently pursuing a master’s degree in Natural Language Processing at a university in Dublin.

I have a strong interest in front-end and back-end development, system architecture, algorithms, game development, and generative AI (such as Stable Diffusion and ChatGPT). I am constantly striving to improve my programming skills, and I enjoy experimenting with new technologies to stay attuned to industry developments.

If you share interests in technology or lifestyle, feel free to reach out to me. We can discuss and exchange ideas together.

Random Thoughts

I know you may be curious about me, so I let ChatGPT ask me some questions and I’ll answer them. Let’s see if these questions satisfy your curiosity.

What is your favorite programming language and why?

I like Go. It is simple, modern, and efficient. Its design philosophy is “less is more”—a very restrained approach, which is something I admire in a language.

What hobbies do you have outside of work and study?

Honestly, I’m kind of a boring person and often don’t know what to do with my time. Back in school, my favorite hobby was gaming. I loved playing all kinds of games, especially PC single-player games. But after I started working, I haven’t had the time to continue with these hobbies.

What is your favorite movie or TV show and why?

I rarely use the word “favorite.” In fact, if you really appreciate most works, they all have their shining moments. However, the movie that left the deepest impression on me is “Blade Runner 2049.” In the end, K lies on the snowy steps, slowly closing his eyes. That moment was filled with loneliness and reflection, and I deeply resonated with it.

What is the biggest challenge as an engineer?

I think the biggest challenge is people—how to manage oneself and how to deal with others. Engineering and science always have solutions, but there is no perfect solution when it comes to people. Fortunately, I’ve always managed to tackle this challenge.

Why did you choose to study in Ireland? How has this experience impacted you?

Initially, my goal was the United States, but I later changed my mind and began considering other countries, especially English-speaking ones like Australia, Canada, and New Zealand. Ireland has many tech companies and is closely connected to the entire EU market, so I ultimately chose Ireland.

I can’t pinpoint the exact impact, but this experience is significant. It has completely changed my life.

How do you relax and relieve stress?

Haha, good question. I always thought I didn’t need to relax and could handle any amount of stress. Until recently, I realized I needed to manage stress better. So, I started trying mindfulness and meditation, hoping these would help alleviate stress.

What three words best represent your personality?

It’s hard to summarize in just three words, so let’s take a different approach.

I think the MBTI is more comprehensive. My personality type is a mix of INTJ and INTP.

In terms of cognitive functions, I possess strong Introverted Intuition (Ni) and Introverted Thinking (Ti). I don’t see the current world; Ni leads me to always see the future outcomes. Everything in my eyes points to a probabilistic view, and I see countless possibilities interwoven like Schrödinger’s cat in superposition. I focus more on internal intuition, future possibilities, and the deeper meaning of the world.

Ti makes me question everything, always in pursuit of the truth. I try to discern the weight of each possibility, figuring out potential paths that lead to a hopeful future.

My Extroverted Thinking (Te) and Extroverted Intuition (Ne) are also quite decent. Ne helps me expand my thoughts, adapt to changes, and socialize while exploring the essence of the world.

Te keeps me efficient, goal-oriented, and prevents excessive procrastination.

My Introverted Feeling (Fi) is relatively strong and supports the value judgments behind my technical cognitive functions. Combined with Ni, it helps me understand what others are thinking during key moments, slightly enhancing my social skills.

My Extroverted Feeling (Fe) is decent enough; after all, we’re all adults. Even if Fe is weak and suppressed by Ti, it eventually gets some training.

My weakest function might be Extraverted Sensing (Se). I seem to be disconnected from reality, and sometimes when I wake up from a dream, I realize I’m actually in a foreign country, and I get a sense of disorientation as if I’ve forgotten that I’ve left home.

A weak Se makes me ignore present experiences, lacking in sensory perception and awareness of physical needs. Strong Ni prevents me from perceiving the present; I’m always living in different futures—happy and joyful because of the future, fearful and anxious because of the future, but never because of the present.

My Introverted Sensing (Si) is also weak. I dislike numerous details; if I can see an abstract and simplified world through Ni, why bother understanding all the tedious details?

Low Si makes me dislike traditions and rarely learn from past experiences. I trust my intuition and believe past mistakes don’t matter. So, I often need to suffer several setbacks to learn a lesson.

How do you define success?

Knowing what you want, striving for it, and ultimately achieving it—that’s success. I am still searching for what I truly want, but sometimes I feel like the goal isn’t clear. Although I have achieved many goals, I am still exploring. Maybe others see me as successful, but I believe there’s much more to explore.

What are your plans or goals for the future?

Maybe I’m a bit conventional, but at this stage, I just want to find the right person to spend my life with. Everything else may be to make this result even better.

Haha, ridiculous, right? It seems like I’ve always gotten it backward. People should live for themselves first and then for others, but I seem to always get it wrong—I achieve a lot first, then think about what I need. So, I envy those who truly live for themselves.

Do you like traveling? If you could travel anywhere, where would you go?

I do, as long as the person I’m with enjoys it, I will too.

If I could, I’d go to the moon. I’d probably sit there watching the beautiful Earth. Maybe someday, when I’ve let go of all obsessions, I’d take off my oxygen mask—but I think that day will never come!
